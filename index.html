About Amanda Dworsky

Founder of Authentic Path Advising

With more than 20 years of experience guiding students through the college admissions process, Amanda Dworsky has built her career around helping young people find their direction and confidence as they plan for life after high school.

As an active counselor for a local high school, Amanda works closely with students and families every day—helping them clarify their goals, build balanced college lists, and navigate each step of the admissions journey. Her approach is both personal and practical, combining expert guidance with genuine care for each student’s individual story.

Before launching Authentic Path Advising, Amanda gained extensive experience on the college side of the admissions process, serving as a Regional Recruiter for the University of Alabama and as an Admissions Director for several colleges in New York. This unique background gives her a rare perspective—she understands what colleges are really looking for and how students can best present themselves.

Amanda has also developed scholarship programs, coordinated dual-enrollment and Advanced Placement initiatives, and led workshops on essays, interviews, and financial aid. She believes that every student has an authentic path waiting to be discovered—and her goal is to help them find it with confidence and clarity.

She holds a Master’s in Professional School Counseling from Sage Graduate School, a Bachelor’s in Public Relations from Syracuse University, and has been a licensed professional school counselor in North Carolina since 2009. Amanda is an active member of professional organizations including the North Carolina School Counselors Association, Southern Association of College Admissions Counselors, and the Lake Norman Consortium of College Counselors.

At Authentic Path Advising, Amanda’s mission is simple: to guide students with honesty, empathy, and expertise—helping them discover their authentic path toward college and career success.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are the top questions we get from families.

  • What exactly do you do as a college counselor?
    We guide students and families through the entire post-secondary planning process — balanced school list, essay coaching, application strategy, timelines, and financial aid basics.
  • When should we start—8th, 9th, 10th, 11th grade?
    We can jump in to guide your student anywhere they are in their high school journey. Many start in 10th–11th for list building, test strategy, and essays. Seniors are welcome for focused support. However, starting in 8th grade you can begin to develop a roadmap for high school.
  • Do you offer virtual or in-person meetings?
    Both. Virtual nationwide and in-person in the Charlotte / Lake Norman area when schedules allow.
  • How do packages and pricing work?
    Choose hourly help, an “Essentials” bundle (essays + list), or comprehensive support. We’ll recommend a scope after a free consult.
  • Do you write essays for students?
    No. We coach brainstorming, structure, voice, and editing so the essay remains the student’s authentic work.
